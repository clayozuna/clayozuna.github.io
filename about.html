About Me

Howdy! I'm Clay.

I'm an electrical engineer, aspiring translator, and photographer. I'm passionate about hands-on electrical projects, global events, and sociology.

I graduated May 8th, 2020 from Texas A&M University with a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering. I spent my summer studying for the NCEES Fundamentals of Engineering exam, which I took and passed September 1, 2020. Currently, I'm looking for a job related to small-signal circuit analysis or power systems.

During Summer 2019, I was an Electrical Engineering intern with Mortenson on a wind turbine farm in Maryville, MO. I made the most of my internship, learning from the substation electrical engineer about assembly and analysis. I also learned from craft workers and field engineers about the timeline of a wind turbine, from site mapping to energization.

In Spring 2018, I became involved in research under Dr. Davis to learn about applying my power systems knowledge using Matlab. In our research, we developed a simulation of the grid of the Bryan/College Station area in Matlab. Then, simulating a blackout, we tested the feasibility of using electric vehicles to help restart the grid. My partner Claire and I used the project as our capstone to write our senior thesis, present findings, and become Undergraduate Research Scholars.

During Spring and Summer 2018, I had a hand in shaping the Texas A&M freshman engineering cirriculm. I worked as a pilot student (recommend by professors) evaluating and providing feedback on new freshman engineering courses that were implemented Fall 2018.

Throughout my time at Texas A&M, I had the opportunity to study abroad twice. Summer 2017 I studied Electric & Magnetic fields and Random Signals & Systems at RWTH University in Germany. During Spring 2020, I finished my degree at Texas A&M University at Qatar.

In addition to electrical engineering I have taken courses in business and entrepreneurship to round out my education. I was also heavily involved with organizations like the Texas A&M Singing Cadets and the MSC to ensure I learned skills not taught in the classroom, such as communication and team dynamics.

I was born and raised in Houston, TX and am a proud graduate from Clear Lake High school. Throughout High school, I was part of the Fighting Falcon Band and the tennis team. Through Project Lead the Way (PLTW), I was able to take engineering courses throughout High school ranging from aerospace engineering to architecture. Through PLTW, I had exposure to soldering circuits, using AutoCAD, and capstone projects well before entering college.

In my free time during the global pandemic, I enjoy playing football (soccer), League of Legends, and repurposing circuits from old appliances. I also enjoy photography. All of the images on this website were taken by me on my travels with my iPhone 6s.

My Hobbies
