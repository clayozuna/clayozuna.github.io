Overview

Clay E. Ozuna

Howdy! I'm Clay Ozuna, a software developer, runner, and world traveler. I'm passionate about website development, being involved on campus, and photography.

Currently I'm a senior at Texas A&M University studying Electrical Engineering with a focus in power.

During summer 2018, I worked as a pilot student at Texas A&M University evaluating the newest freshman engineering courses that will be implemented Fall 2018. I have also been working with my team to put on the 66th MSC Fall Leadership Conference while taking classes. Last summer I studied Electric & Magnetic fields and Random Signals & Systems at RWTH University in Germany.

At Texas A&M, I am Chair of MSC Bethancourt & former chair of the MSC Fall Leadership Conference. I am a Texas A&M Singing Cadet and a member of the Texas A&M Polish Student Association. I also study under Dr. Kate Davis as an undergraduate researcher on the Cyber-Physical Modeling & Assessment team. In addition to electrical engineering, power, and music, I have taken courses in business and entrepreneurship to round out my education.

I was born and raised in Houston, TX and graduated from Clear Lake High School. During my high school time, I took several engineering courses and played trombone in the Clear Lake Falcon Band.

In my free time, I enjoy running, traveling the world, and coding. I also enjoy photography. All of the images on the website were taken by me on my travels.

My Hobbies

When I'm not working on schoolwork, you can find me either coding, learning about other cultures, or running. I am currently signed up to run the ULTRA 55 in December, put on by the Snowdrop Foundation to raise money towards pediatric cancer research.

Summer 2018 I had the opportunity to study abroad in Germany. I studied Electromagnetics and Random Signals and Systems at RWTH, a university in Western Germany. During free time, I traveled to the Netherlands, Poland, Scotland, and the Czech Republic. Most memorable was a solo cross-country train trip from Western Germany to Southern Poland that I organized on my own. I met several people along the way, some I still keep in contact with.

I have been coding ever since I was young, working with LEGO Mindstorm robots and going up from there. In highschool, I took 5 PLTW engineering courses where I learned how to work with VEX robotics in addition to other engineering fundamentals in aerospace, civil, and electrical enginering. In college I learned HTML, CSS, C++, Matlab, and Python the first couple of years. Now, I am branching out and experimenting with Javascript while refining what I currently know.
