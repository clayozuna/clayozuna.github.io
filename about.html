Overview

Clay E. Ozuna

Howdy! I'm Clay Ozuna, a software developer, runner, and world traveler. I'm passionate about website development, being involved on campus, and photography.

Currently I'm a senior at Texas A&M University studying Electrical Engineering with a focus in power.

During summer 2018, I worked as a pilot student at Texas A&M University evaluating the newest freshman engineering courses that will be implemented Fall 2018. I have also been working with my team to put on the 66th MSC Fall Leadership Conference while taking classes. Last summer I studied Electric & Magnetic fields and Random Signals & Systems at RWTH University in Germany.

At Texas A&M, I am Chair of the MSC Fall Leadership Conference & MSC Bethancourt. I am a Texas A&M Singing Cadet and a member of the Texas A&M Polish Student Association. I also study under Dr. Kate Davis as an undergraduate researcher on the Cyber-Physical Modeling & Assessment team. In addition to electrical engineering, power, and music, I have taken courses in business and entrepreneurship.

I was born and raised in Houston, TX and graduated from Clear Lake High School. During my high school time, I took several engineering courses and played trombone in the Clear Lake Falcon Band.

In my free time, I enjoy traveling the world, photography, learning french, coding, swimming, and investing in startups.
