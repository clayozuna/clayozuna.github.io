Course work

ENGR 111 was my first college engineering course. In the course, we learned how to draft by hand using compasses, protractors, and engineering scales. It provided me with a hands-on expereince of what it was like to draft projects before Autodesk. The best learning experience from the course was the Rube Goldberg machine. A Rube Goldberg machine is many simple machines like a lever put together to perform a simple task. We formed groups the first class, a room full of freshman students, and worked on designing it the entire semester using skills learned from drafting and AutoCad. Our final project was a 5'x 5'x 5' machine with 16 steps that could open an umbrella. Our budget was $100 and we kept the overall design under $60 while winning the most creative machine in class. From the project I learned how to work with team members you've never met before.

ECEN 325 taught me circuit analysis. The course taught me how to work with Bode plots, diodes, op-amps, BJTs, and MOSFETs. What I enjoyed most about the course is that lab revolves around using a kit to construct circuits that we have solved for the transfer function, Bode plots, and components at certain frequencies. Last week we solved for and constructed second order lowpass, highpass, and bandpass filters and measured input and output using WaveForms. Simulations were created and tested on MultiSim then compared with the actuals.

In ECEN 454, another course I'm currently taking, we are focused on digital integrated circuit design. The course currently revolves around creating simple circuit components like a NAND or XOR gate from the transistor level using pMOS and nMOS. We are currently designing these components in Cadence.
